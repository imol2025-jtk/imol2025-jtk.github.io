The 7th International Workshop on Intrinsically Motivated Open-ended Learning (IMOL 2025)

8–10 September 2025 • University of Hertfordshire, Hatfield, UK

Why IMOL 2025?

Current AI technologies achieve remarkable success in specific tasks, yet struggle in open-ended, unpredictable environments. IMOL research aims to bridge this gap by investigating mechanisms inspired by intrinsic motivation—those that enable humans and animals to explore, learn, and develop autonomously through self-generated goals and internal drives.

IMOL 2025 will bring together researchers from AI, machine learning, robotics, cognitive science, psychology, biology, and related disciplines to share and discuss the latest advancements in intrinsically motivated and open-ended learning. The workshop will showcase cross-disciplinary research that addresses the challenges of understanding and building autonomous, adaptive systems, focusing on theoretical foundations, algorithmic innovations, cognitive and biological insights, and real-world applications of open-ended learning.

Workshop Details

  • Workshop Location: University of Hertfordshire, Hatfield, UK (≈25 min by fast train from London King’s Cross)
  • Workshop Dates: September 8–10, 2025
  • Submission Deadline: July 13, 2025
  • Registration: Required, but free of charge
